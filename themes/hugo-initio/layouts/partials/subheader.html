I’m a fisheries researcher for the Sustainable Fisheries Groupat the University of California, Santa Barbara (UCSB). Most of my recent work has been with bioeconomic modeling of tuna fisheries in the Pacific Ocean. I earned my Masters in Environmental Science and Management (MESM) in 2016 from the Bren School of Environmental Science and Managementat UCSB. My thesis was on the effects of seafood certifications in small-scale fisheries. Prior to that I worked as a Waterfront Assistant at the School for Field Studies’ Center for Marine Resource Studies in the Turks and Caicos Islands and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Biochemistry and Environmental Science from the University of San Diego in 2013.
